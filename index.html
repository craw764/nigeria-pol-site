About the Candidate

Kene Nnadi, Esq.

Barrister & Solicitor · NDC Candidate, 2027 General Elections

At 38, Kene Nnadi is a distinguished Barrister and passionate community advocate who is stepping forward in the 2027 General Elections to bring the rigour of legal expertise, integrity, and servant-leadership to the House of Representatives on behalf of the people of Enugu South and Enugu North.

Born and raised in Enugu State, Kene rose through hard work and academic excellence — graduating from the prestigious University of Nigeria, Nsukka where he studied Law. He was subsequently called to the Nigerian Bar and has spent over 14 years in legal practice, championing justice for individuals, businesses, and communities in Enugu State. His work in public interest litigation and community legal aid has earned him deep respect across the constituency.

Education & Skill Development

Youth & Job Creation

Driving legislation that unlocks economic opportunities for young Enugu people — from startup grants to federal job placement and digital economy initiatives.

  • Youth enterprise fund legislation
  • Tech & digital skills hub for Enugu

Infrastructure & Roads

Advocating for federal roads, bridges, power supply, and urban renewal projects that modernise Enugu South and Enugu North and connect them to national economic corridors.

  • Federal road rehabilitation in Enugu
  • Power infrastructure upgrade
  • Urban renewal & drainage works

Healthcare Access

Sponsoring bills to bring world-class federal medical facilities, essential drug supply, and maternal health programmes to underserved communities across Enugu.

  • Federal PHC upgrade across Enugu
  • Free maternal & child health care
  • Mobile health outreach programme

Rule of Law & Justice

As a Barrister, Kene will champion legislation that strengthens the judiciary, protects citizens' rights, and ensures access to justice for the poorest Nigerians.

  • Legal aid fund for the indigent

  • Speedy justice reform bill

Security & Community Safety

Working with federal security agencies to resource police posts, deploy surveillance infrastructure, and build community policing partnerships that keep Enugu families safe.
